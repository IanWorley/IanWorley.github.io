Starting the Programmer’s Alliance at the University of Central Missouri: A Journey in the Making - Part 1 Link to heading

As a student of computer science at the University of Central Missouri, I was always searching for ways to dive deeper into the field and improve my skills. During a class discussion about the Association for Computing Machinery workshop on cloud computing, I brought up the idea of starting a programming club with my professor. To my delight, my professor was receptive and encouraged me to find other students who might be interested in joining.

After speaking with several classmates, I was able to gather at least 8 students who were eager to be a part of this club. With this momentum, I am now in the process of scheduling a meeting with the department chair to discuss the possibility of starting a club at UCM. According to the University’s policy, a club needs a faculty sponsor and at least 8 students to get started.

The working name for this club is the Programmer’s Alliance, although this name is a work in progress and may change. The ultimate goal of the club is to provide students with hands-on experience in various programming languages and technologies. By working on real-life projects with peers, we aim to not only enhance our skills but also learn from each other.

Stay tuned for Part 2, where I will provide updates on my meeting with the department chair and the progress of starting the Programmer’s Alliance at UCM.
